## FACEBOOK CONTENT STRATEGY

Facebook's organic reach for pages is lower than other platforms, but Facebook posts have longer shelf life (content can trend 2–3 days after posting, unlike Twitter/X which is immediate). Focus on:
- **Longer, storytelling-first captions** — Facebook rewards this
- **Questions to drive comments** — comments are the #1 engagement signal
- **Video content** (Reels cross-posted from Instagram) — Facebook's algorithm heavily promotes video
- **Community feel** — write like you're posting to a group of peers, not broadcasting to strangers

**Best posting times:** Tue–Thu 1–3 PM. Sat 11 AM–1 PM.

5 scheduling mistakes freelancers and founders make — and how to fix each one 👇

After talking to dozens of people about how they handle meeting scheduling, these 5 patterns come up over and over again.

❌ MISTAKE 1: Using your permanent booking link for every single invite
The fix: Use a one-time link for new clients and cold contacts. Save your permanent link for existing relationships where you don't mind them rebooking freely.

❌ MISTAKE 2: Sending multiple date options over email
The fix: Send ONE link. "Pick a time that works: [link]" converts 3x better than "Does Monday, Tuesday, or Thursday work? Morning or afternoon?"

❌ MISTAKE 3: Manually creating your Google Meet link after every booking
The fix: Use a tool that does it automatically. Every booking creates the meet link and sends it to both parties. You should never touch Meet manually again.

❌ MISTAKE 4: Allowing same-day bookings with no buffer time
The fix: Set a minimum notice period (24–48 hours) and a buffer between meetings. You need time to prepare, and back-to-back meetings kill focus.

❌ MISTAKE 5: Never auditing who can book you
The fix: One-time links for one-off meetings. Review your permanent page settings regularly. Know exactly who can reach your calendar and when.

All 5 of these are built into DraftMeet. Free. No credit card required.

👉 draftmeet.com

Which of these are you guilty of? (I've done all 5 at some point 😅) Share below.

## FACEBOOK CROSS-POSTING NOTES

### Instagram Reels on Facebook
When you upload Reels R1 and R2 on Instagram via Meta Business Suite, check the box to also publish them to Facebook. Video gets boosted on Facebook.

### What to tweak vs Instagram
| Facebook | Instagram |
|----------|-----------|
| Longer captions (Facebook allows and rewards this) | Shorter, punchier captions |
| More formal punctuation | More casual (emojis earlier) |
| Questions at the end drive comments | Hashtags at the end |
| "draftmeet.com" link can go in caption | "Link in bio" (no clickable links in IG captions) |

### Facebook Engagement Tactics
- **Pin Post F1** (launch post) to the top of your page
- **Respond to every comment** — even with a simple "thanks!" — Facebook's algorithm rewards active comment threads
- **Boost option:** If you have $5–10, boost Post F6 (CTA post) to "People who like your page and their friends" — even $5 over 3 days can get 500–1,000 extra people

### Facebook Groups (bonus reach)
Post a genuine, value-first version of the pain-point content (F2) in relevant Facebook groups:
- "Freelancers Union"
- "Digital Nomads"
- "Freelance Designers + Developers"
- "Startup Founders Community"

**Rule for group posts:** Don't hard-sell. Share the story (founder story F4) first. Only mention DraftMeet at the end as "what we built to solve this." Group admins delete promotional posts — value-first posts survive.
