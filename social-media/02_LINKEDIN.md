5 scheduling mistakes freelancers make (and how to fix each one):

❌ Mistake 1: Sending your Calendly link to everyone
Fix: Use a one-time link for new clients. Permanent pages for established relationships. Don't expose your main calendar to cold strangers.

❌ Mistake 2: Offering 3+ date options over email
Fix: Send a single scheduling link. "Pick a time here" converts better than "does Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday work for you? Morning or afternoon?"

❌ Mistake 3: Creating the Google Meet link yourself
Fix: Automate it. Every minute you spend manually creating meeting links is a minute you're not billing. Use a tool that does it on booking.

❌ Mistake 4: No buffer between meetings
Fix: Set minimum booking notice (24–48 hours) and buffer time between slots. You need time to prepare and decompress.

❌ Mistake 5: No expiry on proposal links
Fix: One-time booking links expire after a single use. This stops a prospect from rebooking your time without permission months later.

All 5 of these are built into DraftMeet. Free. No credit card.

## LINKEDIN PERFORMANCE NOTES

- **Best posting times:** Mon–Thu 8–10 AM and 12–1 PM. Avoid Fri after 2 PM and weekends.
- **Engagement tactic:** Reply to every comment within the first 2 hours. LinkedIn rewards active conversations.
- **Dwell time:** LinkedIn tracks how long people read your post. Long-form posts (700–1,300 chars) perform better than short ones.
- **The "see more" hook:** The first 3 lines are shown before the "see more" cut-off. Make them irresistible — this is your headline.
- **Never link in post body.** Always first comment. LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes posts with external links.
- **Tag sparingly.** Only tag people if they're genuinely referenced. Tag spam kills reach.
