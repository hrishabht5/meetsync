📅 Facebook Content Rules

✓
Post 3–4× per week on your Page. Best times: Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11am and 1–3pm local time.
✓
Always include an image or video. Text-only posts get ~50% less reach on Facebook. Use a branded graphic from Canva for every post.
✓
Use Facebook Stories. Post a Story daily — it's separate from the feed algorithm and keeps your page visible.
✓
Ask questions. Facebook's algorithm heavily boosts posts that generate comments. End every post with a question.
✓
Groups: 90/10 rule. Be helpful 90% of the time. Only mention DraftMeet when it naturally solves someone's stated problem.
✓
Boost your launch post with even $5–10 of Facebook Ads once you have 3–5 comments — the algorithm will amplify a post that already has engagement.
💡 Facebook audience: Skews 25–45, small business owners, consultants, and freelancers. Slightly older and more business-focused than Instagram. Write in plain, direct language — no tech jargon. Lead with the time-saving benefit, not the feature.

Key Rules

Cross-Platform Golden Rules

1
Post every day on Twitter. Twitter requires daily presence. Miss 3 days and your reach drops significantly.
2
LinkedIn 3× per week from personal profile. Company pages alone get minimal reach. Your personal account is the engine.
3
Reply to every comment within 1 hour of posting. Every platform rewards early engagement velocity.
4
Build in public. Share waitlist numbers, behind the scenes, bugs you fixed, features you shipped. Founders love founders.
5
One primary CTA only. Every post ends with one clear action: "Join the waitlist at draftmeet.com" — never multiple asks.
6
Adapt content, don't copy-paste. Same core message, but written natively for each platform's tone and format.
7
Hashtags: Twitter (3–5 relevant), Instagram (10–15 mixed size), LinkedIn (3–5 professional). No hashtags on Reddit.
